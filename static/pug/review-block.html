I didn't like the format. It would be much better to alternate practical lessons with theoretical ones. Here everything goes in blocks: 6-7 theoretical lectures are replaced by 6-7 practical tasks. Let's say you, like me, are a complete zero in programming (namely, for such people, this course, at least that's how it is written). For you, any new information is a whole world that needs to be understood and mastered. And right there they give you a huge amount of theory on the forehead, without fixing it with any small practical task. And then you just sit for hours on the practice, revising the same theory 6-8 times. Sometimes, by the way, the explanations are not very clear. In parallel, I look at other people's explanations on YouTube - and it really comes better.

It would also be a good idea to have something like a synopsis at the end of each section, squeezing out the most important information.

And even in practical tasks, the things that were taught are not always considered directly. As, for example, in the section with functions and with classes.
